A7: Final Project Proposal

Last updated: Mon, Nov 21, 2022

For my final project, I will create a custom chess set which includes a chess board
and 32 chess pieces. I plan to use the following digital fabrication techniques:

  • 3D modeling in Rhino and modifying meshes of sourced chess pieces.
  • Subtractive manufacturing to create panels with connector joints.
  • Laser cutting wood panels to be stacked on top of each other.
  • Molding and casting individual pieces with high quality plaster.



---

I. Concept fabrication & breakdown of tasks

Here's a moodboard of the chess set I plan to make:



For the exterior of the board, I will 3D print rectangular side panels and a square bottom surface
that are connected via boxjoints, and then I will sand the edges.

For the interior, I will laser cut square sheets of wood to be stacked on top of each other to give
the board some depth and weight. I'm also considering leaving some space in the middle to add a metal
weight in case the stacked wood doesn't feel heavy enough.

For the squares on top, I'm considering laser cutting wooden squares in two contrasting colors
and then gluing them together.



For the chess pieces, I will iterate my work in the previous assignment and use molding and casting techniques.
I anticipate this to be the most time-consuming portion of the project because there's... a lot.

In chess, there are 6 unique pieces and 32 pieces altogether.
There are 16 pawns, 4 knights, 4 bishops, 4 rooks, 2 queens, and 2 kings.

After sourcing meshes of chess pieces online, I will design and 3D print molds of the 6 unique pieces.
Once each 3D printed mold is finished, I will sand the interior to ensure a smooth finish when casting silicone.

When casting silicone, I'll use the leftover oomoo I have from the previous assignment.
I'm hopeful I'll have enough because I really don't want to spend another $40.

Lastly, I will cast each individual piece in resin using the silicone molds.
This will be a looooong process because I need to account for 16 pawn pieces.
If I have even more leftover oomoo, I'll cast 2 seperate pawn molds out of silicone to make it go by faster.




---

II. Timeline with contingency plans, & bill of materials with sourcing schedule

Contingencies:

  1. If the Mill's laser cutters are down, I will not include the wooden interior since that element
    is mostly just to provide depth and weight to the board. For the top squares however, this means
    I'd have to 3D print each square individually instead of laser cutting wood. This also means I'd
    have to print another surface for the pieces to sit flat on.

  2. If molding and casting somehow fails, I might resort to 3D printing the pieces in resin at the Mill.
    I realize that casting resin also takes a long time, so I'm considering looking for a more lightweight material
    that cures quicker and isn't as expensive. If I don't finish my molds by next Tuesday, I will consider
    making the switch to 3D printing to just get pieces on the board, or maybe even create laser cut wood that are
    press fit together. Now that I'm typing this out, though, I kinda like this alternative more than molding and casting.

  3. If I'm running low on any materials, I'm also considering scaling the set and pieces down in size to
    to create more of a handheld or portable set. Chess board dimensions typically float around 20x20 inches
    (2.5x2.5 inch squares). To save on material and focus more on execution, I might scale it down to either
    12x12 inches or 8x8 inches.

  4. If my 3D printer dies, I will be very sad! (and go to the Mill)
