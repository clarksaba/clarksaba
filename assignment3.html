A3: Getting Started with 3D Printing

Last updated: Tues, Oct 25, 2022

For this assignment, I assembled and callibrated my 3D printer and printed basic shapes with different settings in Cura.
After experimenting with the various options, I designed a nested model in Rhino to slice and print.



---

I. Setting up my 3D printer

Assembling my Ender 3 Pro took around an hour and a half to complete.

To properly align the bed, I followed the Bed Leveling Protocol by Filament Friday.

At first, my bed was too far from the extruder because I didn't own the tempered glass bed -
so I adjusted the z-switch to be lower, and then I adjusted the bed screws. Using a thin piece
of paper, I checked for resistance with the extruder at each corner and the center of the bed.

II. Printing small basic shapes

To get comfortable with 3D printing, I started off with modeling (in Rhino) and slicing (with Cura) small basic shapes.



First, I modeled a 2cm cube in Rhino, exported it as a .stl file and then imported that into Cura.
I sliced three cubes at low quality, standard quality and super quality, then sliced another with a
concentric top and bottom. Respectively, these prints took 17, 22, 38 and 18 minutes.



Then, I modeled and imported a 3cm diameter/height tube and experimented with wall thickness settings.
I sliced one tube with .4mm wall thickness and another with .8mm wall thickness. It's hard to notice these
differences in the physical prints, but the Cura preview reveals a clearer distinction in the layering of the wall.
Additionally, I changed the z-seam alignment on the double thickness tube from user specified to random.
I assume this is what causes the wall texture to appear spotty since the starting point in each layer is different.

The cylinder with single extrusion wall thickness took 24 minutes to print and resulted in a height of 29.85mm
and a width of 29.91mm. The cylinder with double extrusion wall thickness and random z-seam alignment took
30 minutes to print and resulted in a height of 29.26mm and a width of 30.38mm.



I moved onto cylinders that were also 3cm diameter/height.

I exported the first cylinder from Rhino into an .stl file and specified a 0.1mm tolerance.
This slice lasted 27 minutes with a height of 30.06mm and a width of 29.69mm.



I exported the second cylinder and specified a 0.001mm tolerance.
This slice lasted 27 minutes with a height of 30.03mm and a width of 29.99mm.



For the third cylinder I specified the setting in Cura to spiralize the outer contour.
This slice lasted 22 minutes with a height of 30.26mm and a width of 29.45mm.
This setting has a faster print time because it only prints one wall with 0 infill.



Lastly, I rotated a cylinder in Cura to be on its side and added support.
This slice lasted 25 minutes with a height of 29.5mm and a width of 29.7mm.
This cylinder in particular was a headache because laying it on its side meant the
round walls were being extruded horizontally and thus were less precise.





---

III. Modeling and printing nested parts

In Rhino, I created a series of rings out of extruded tubes that were then rotated and scaled down.
I went ahead and placed an eyeball in the middle that I found for free on an .stl sharing website
because the model started to remind me of a biblically accurate angel.



The challenge was getting these rings to float altogether as a printed model and not fall apart.
I inserted these connectors between each ring by extruding and trimming small tubes.



Here's a completed Rhino model of the nested rings (and eyeball) with connectors holding it together.



My first attempt at printing a V1 failed because I added way too much support in Cura which resulted in
an extremely long duration and a print that would not detach itself from the support.


I did manage to get these nested rings, but I wanted to keep experimenting with settings until I was successful.



For V2, I rotated the model upright instead of laying it flat, and I removed the support. This drastically reduced
the print time to 25 minutes (but also because I scaled the model down because I was running out of PLA).

Although the print came out in one piece and was held together, it was a mess of webs.



At this point I ran out of PLA, so I ordered more in black.

For V3, I scaled the model up and printed in super quality.
