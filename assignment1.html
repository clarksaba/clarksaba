A1: Laser Cut Press Fit Construction Kit

Last updated: Tues, Oct 11, 2022

For this assignment, I wanted to construct a soccer ball by laser cutting cardboard shapes that were measured and modeled in 3D using Rhino.

Did I succeed? We'll see later this week after I visit the maker space.

But for now, let's talk about the modeling process.



---

I. Fabricating the idea in Rhino (☉_☉) (my first time 3d modeling)

After browsing a lot of press fit construction kits online, I was fascinated by the more circular designs and settled on creating a soccer ball.

The assignment required 30 instances of shapes, so I thought it was a nice fit (haha) that a soccer ball consists of 12 pentagons and 20 hexagons for a total of 32 instances.

However... a challenge was that these pieces were being press fitted together at an angle.

To make sure the physical construction doesn't fall apart, I created multiple insertion slots on each side of every shape.



---

II. Creating laser cutting batches in Illustrator (-‿◦)

One laser cutting batch contains 6 pentagons and 10 hexagons — half of the shapes needed to construct the ball.
